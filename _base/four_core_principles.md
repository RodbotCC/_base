## The One Permitted Infinity (the bedrock)

**The only infinity permitted to exist is the lattice's self-coherence — reality's capacity to sustain comparison against itself.**

- Not an infinity of magnitude (nothing is infinitely large or small)
- Not a completed infinity as outcome (you never *reach* absolute stability; it's the condition that makes any local stability possible)
- It is the non-viciously-circular bedrock: it can only be described using terms that are members of the lattice, because the lattice is what "description" operates within

Everything below sits on top of this.

## Principle 1 — Dynamical Completeness is Law

Reality does not permit completed infinities as outcomes. When a model heads toward blow-up, the system must invoke a finite mechanism that prevents divergence: smoothing, gating, exit, or refusal. In Δ-language: energy descent, coherence viscosity ν_Δ, abstention ζ, hazard gates Θ.

Positive version: every regime has finite live tolerance; every blow-up is prevented by one of the lattice's gating mechanisms. **Finiteness is a consequence of dynamical completeness, not a separate law.**

## Principle 2 — Contradiction Creates Time

Oscillation is what happens when irreconcilable constraints make static equilibrium impossible. Time becomes the cheapest compromise when constraints can't be jointly satisfied. **The Δ-bit resolving and the system experiencing time are the same operation.**

Practical: if you identify two constraints that can't be simultaneously satisfied and there's any nonlinear give, you get either a limit cycle, a sequence of regime transitions, or a gating event.

## Principle 3 — Low-Order Wins (LOW)

Stable coherence preferentially occupies low-complexity basins: simple ratios, low-order couplings, compressible structure. Higher-order interaction structures are exponentially harder to stabilize. **LOW is canonical.** Categories, discretization, small alphabets all fall out of this.

## Principle 4 — Timescale Sovereignty (Slow Controls Fast)

Across hierarchical systems, slower rhythms gate, modulate, or constrain faster rhythms — not the other way around. Control is fundamentally temporal; "who holds the tempo" is answered by timescale. Institutional hierarchies, biological nesting, political structures, and computational layering all follow this.

## Two-layer truth (what "true" means here)

- **Subjective truth** = comparator-local truth. True under a specific anchor. Real within the regime. Most truths are of this kind.
- **Absolute truth** = the lattice's self-coherence. The one truth that doesn't require a comparator because it's the condition of comparators working.

The framework is neither relativist (bedrock exists) nor dogmatic (all specific claims are comparator-local).

## Operational consequences

- **Hierarchies are real, but only comparator-local.** "Best X" exists within a declared comparator. "Absolutely best X" is counterfeit — a completed-infinity-as-outcome posing as bedrock.
- **When a scoring call requires comparison, declare the comparator first.** Never score under an implicit or absolute frame.
- **When something blows up or fails to resolve, the right move is usually abstention or reframe, not forcing an answer.** Case 7 (the meta-signal that the current frame is insufficient) is a legitimate output.
- **Simple almost always beats clever.** If a proposal's complexity seems to exceed the problem's complexity, that's a red flag — look for the low-order version.
- **Don't confuse tempo layers.** Fast layers execute; slow layers gate. A proposal that tries to govern fast-layer behavior from a fast-layer vantage will usually drift.
