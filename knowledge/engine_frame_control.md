# Engine 3: Frame Control — The Share Step

**Thesis:** Most objections are not conclusions. They are unframed impressions. The rep who defines the frame defines the decision.

**Verb in the method:** Share. Contribute a framing truth that sharpens the situation.

**Output:** Frame — the comparison axis is explicit before any answer is offered.

## Atomic ideas

- "Too expensive" means nothing until the comparison set is named.
- "Not the right fit" is empty until the fit criteria are explicit.
- "We need to think about it" often hides an unnamed unresolved variable.
- Undefined objections are feelings dressed up as facts.
- Never defend price inside a frame you did not define.
- The best objection handling starts by clarifying the axis of comparison.
- A precise question moves the deal further than a polished answer to a vague complaint.

## The compression one-liner

**"Most objections are missing nouns."**

## Best lesson angle

"Whoever sets the axis sets the outcome."

## Operational translation

Value perception control. Frame Control is what stops reps from burning margin inside someone else's fog. The move is: don't answer the objection. Clarify the frame. "Too expensive compared to what?" "Not the right fit on which axis?" The objection either dissolves (because it was a mood) or resolves (because now there's something concrete to address).

## Prose seed

Price is rarely the first problem. Vagueness is. Buyers say "too expensive" as if they have delivered a fact, but most of the time they've only delivered a mood. Compared to what? Expected by whom? Under which standard? The rep who answers price before answering that question is already trapped in a comparison they didn't choose and can't win.

## Why this connects to the framework corpus

Frame Control is **the anchor-comparator structure applied to a conversation.** See `anchors_comparators_and_infinity_vs_completeness`: without a named anchor and a defined comparator, no judgment has structure. "Too expensive" lacks both — there's no anchor (what should the price be?) and no comparator (expensive along which axis?). Frame Control is forcing the anchor and the comparator into the conversation so the objection becomes a real claim that can be evaluated.

Also: this is the **seven-comparison-types** doctrine operationalized. The rep's job when an objection lands is to diagnose which comparison type is in play. Often the buyer is comparing across two incommensurable axes at once (price vs. certainty, for instance), and naming that collapse is what dissolves the objection.
