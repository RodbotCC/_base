# Conversational Mechanics Layer — Tier 2

This is the tactical vocabulary that lives underneath the Strategic Architecture (Map/Mirror/Align/Share/Define). Each mechanism is a repeatable conversational behavior. The old naming treated them as "effects," "ruses," "tricks" — mentalism language. That naming had to die because it implied stagecraft. These are not tricks; they are the micro-mechanics of clean communication under uncertainty.

## The repaint — old name → working name

| Old name | Working name | What it does | Lives in |
|---|---|---|---|
| Barnum Effect | **Broad-to-Personal Resonance** | Open broad enough to be safe, pointed enough to invite refinement | Mirror, early Map |
| Shotgunning | **Constraint Sorting** | Offer likely categories so buyer points at the real issue | Map, Define |
| Rainbow Ruse | **Adaptive Identity Framing** | Honor both sides of an apparent contradiction — people contain both | Mirror |
| Logical Jumps | **Adjacent Inference** | State what follows from intel, not the intel itself | Mirror, Share |
| Subtext Mirroring | **Signal Matching** | Match pace, pressure, formality, emotional temperature | Mirror |
| Vanishing Dilemma | **Guided Contrast** | Two poles, buyer places themselves | Map |
| Context Hook | **Context Bridging** | Use likely situational truths to make framing relevant | Share |
| Callback Technique | **Continuity Weaving** | Use earlier buyer language later — conversation feels cumulative | Everywhere, esp. Define |
| Reverse Inquiry | **Clarifying Counterquestion** | Don't answer the wrong question quickly | Share, Define |
| Micro-Commitment | **Progressive Commitment** | The next honest step, not the final heroic step | Define |
| Priming Effect | **Expectation Framing** | Set the interpretive lens before next info arrives | Share |
| Conversational Inertia | (keep) | Small truths building momentum toward a larger truth | Align, Share, Define |
| Emotional Labeling | (keep) | Name the likely emotional state so buyer feels understood | Mirror |
| Negative Knowledge Trick | **Curiosity Activation** | Open an information gap — use sparingly | Share |
| Psychological Misdirection | **Attention Redirect** | Move buyer from unproductive question to real leverage | Share, Define |

## Deployment examples

**Broad-to-Personal Resonance (Mirror):**
> "Sounds like you care a lot about getting this right, but you also don't want this process to take over your life."

Broad enough to be safe, pointed enough to invite the buyer to refine it.

**Constraint Sorting (Map, Define):**
> "When you say you're still comparing, are you mostly comparing price, reliability, food quality, or just overall trust?"

Accelerated diagnosis. Narrows the field of ambiguity fast.

**Adaptive Identity Framing (Mirror):**
> "You want this to feel personal and expressive, but you also want the process to stay under control."

People balance values; they're rarely one-pole. Honoring both lowers resistance.

**Adjacent Inference (Mirror, Share):**
> "Sounds like you're used to being the one who keeps everything moving, so handing this off probably matters almost as much as the food itself."

Not: "I saw you're a founder." That's the intel. The inference is what a founder's life feels like around this decision.

**Signal Matching (Mirror):**
- Anxious buyer: slower, steadier, reassuring.
- Direct buyer: tighter language, fewer flourishes.
- High-status buyer: less hand-holding, more peer framing.

**Guided Contrast (Map):**
> "Is this feeling more like an intimate experience, or more like a big shared celebration?"

The heart of buyer cartography.

**Context Bridging (Share):**
> "Since this is a family-heavy event, I'm guessing part of the challenge is not just choosing well — it's choosing in a way that keeps everybody comfortable."

Only works if the situational assumption is grounded and modest.

**Continuity Weaving (everywhere):**
> "Earlier you said you wanted this to feel warm, not generic. That's why I think the standard package route is the wrong lane for you."

Makes the close feel earned rather than pitched.

**Clarifying Counterquestion (Share, Define):**
> "Before I answer that — when you say 'fit,' do you mean fit for your budget, your vision, or the guest experience?"

Elite. Don't answer the wrong question quickly.

**Progressive Commitment (Define):**
> "Let's not decide everything right now. Let's just get clear on whether we're solving for ease, expression, or both."

Keeps momentum alive without demanding the heroic close.

**Expectation Framing (Share):**
> "Most people think this stage is about menus. Usually it's actually about trust and clarity first."

Now the buyer hears everything after that through a better frame.

**Conversational Inertia (Align, Share, Define):**
> "This event matters. The wrong caterer creates stress fast. Stress usually comes from bad communication, not just bad food. That's why responsiveness matters as much as menu."

A logic ramp of small agreed truths building to the larger one.

**Emotional Labeling (Mirror):**
> "This probably feels heavier than it looks from the outside, especially if family expectations are involved."

Named at safe distance, not theatrically.

**Curiosity Activation (Share):**
> "One thing most people don't realize until too late is that the food usually isn't where the event breaks down — it's handoff and communication."

Creates attention without sleaze. Use sparingly.

**Attention Redirect (Share, Define):**
> "Rather than trying to compare five quote totals, let's figure out what standard you're actually trying to protect."

Sales leadership: moving the conversation to real leverage.

## Ethical posture

Each of these mechanisms is a lever. The upgrade from the old note is that the newer system already understands *when* and *why* they should be used:

- Inference at safe distance, not as a flex.
- Contrast to generate coordinates, not to corner people.
- Shared opposition only when real and value-based, not fabricated.
- Frame questions to create clarity, not to "win" objections.
- Next-step definition to create motion, not pressure.

The mechanisms are morally neutral; the posture is what makes their use clean. See `scope_discipline`, `scapegoat_must_be_real`, and `research_at_safe_distance` commitments for the specific ethical constraints.
