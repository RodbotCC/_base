### Why "alignment with the dimensionless ratio lattice" matters in Jake's statement
He specifically mentioned alignment to the lattice as a requirement — not a decoration. Here's why it matters structurally:

If the system were composed from the ten modules but the modules themselves weren't running on lattice-aligned dynamics (canonical ratios, LOW preference, Delta Limit at each regime boundary, abstention as first-class), it would *simulate* the phenomenology without being genuinely framework-lawful. ### The "imperfectly perfect or perfectly imperfect" phrase is doing real work
Jake included this specifically: bias, imperfection, coherent fallibility. This matters because:

- **Perfect deterministic behavior** would give away that the system is not operating like a real agent
- **Unstructured noise** would give away that it's not coherent under the hood
- **Imperfect coherence** — consistent personality with contextual drift, bias that remains internally consistent, failures that are predictable given the system's actual structure — is the signature of a real operating agent

The framework predicts this directly. Δ-bit resolution under stochastic anticipation fields produces *path-dependent novelty* — each step is lawful but not predictable from initial conditions alone. A correctly-built system would have this texture natively, because it would be the downstream consequence of composing the modules on framework-lawful internals. Not a parameter you tune; a behavior that emerges.
